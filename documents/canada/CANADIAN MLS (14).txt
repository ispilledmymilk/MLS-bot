FCNB COMPLIANCE 
1. The "Licensee Disclosure" Rule
FCNB requires "Full Disclosure" on any internet-related advertising that a consumer can view as a separate unit (e.g., a specific listing page).
* The Rule: Every viewable page of a marketplace must include (or link directly to) the following details:
   1. The licensee’s name as registered with FCNB.
   2. The full name of the brokerage.
   3. The address of the brokerage office.
* Banner Ads: If a thumbnail or banner is too small, it must link to a page containing this full disclosure.
* Reference: FCNB Real Estate Agents Licensing Information
2. Lead Generation vs. Licensed Activity
New Brunswick is very specific about where "tech" ends and "licensed activity" begins.
* Simple Referrals: Your marketplace can collect a name and contact detail (lead) and pass it to an agent without a license.
* The "Pre-Screening" Trap: If your marketplace uses AI or staff to "pre-screen" leads, take applications, or provide advice on property suitability, the FCNB may deem this licensed activity. In New Brunswick, anyone "taking mortgage or real estate information beyond basic contact details" must be licensed.
* Reference: FCNB Bulletin: Mortgage Brokering & Lead Generation
3. Data Integrity & Consent (Written Permission)
FCNB mandates that information provided online must be "current, consistent, and accurate."
* Written Permission: Licensed entities should not advertise other entities' listings on a third-party marketplace without written permission. For your marketplace, this means ensuring your Data Access Agreement (DAA) with the local board is up to date.
* Timely Updates: Material changes to a listing (Price drops, "Sold" status) must be updated in a "timely manner." FCNB generally mirrors the 24-48 hour industry standard for digital syncs.
4. The "Promotional Activity" Amendment (2026)
As of January 1, 2026, New Brunswick's Securities Act and related consumer protection rules were amended to increase penalties for "misleading promotional activities."
* The Rule: If your marketplace uses "suggestive" or "promotional" language that is found to be misleading (e.g., claiming a property is a "guaranteed investment" or using fake scarcity tactics), the FCNB now has expanded authority to issue administrative penalties without a full hearing.
* Reference: FCNB Securities Notice 2026-01: Promotional Activities
5. Sold Prices & Transparency
* Public Assessment vs. Private Sale: While New Brunswick's Property Assessment Online (PAOL) tool shows recent sales prices to the public (usually with a 30-day delay), the FCNB and the New Brunswick Real Estate Association (NBREA) generally restrict brokerages from advertising those same sold prices on public IDX feeds.
* The VOW Solution: To display real-time sold data in NB, your marketplace must use a VOW (Virtual Office Website) registration to comply with privacy expectations and the Real Estate Agents Act.

	New Brunswick (FCNB) Standard
	Brokerage Address
	Must be included in the disclosure (unlike some other provinces).
	Lead Capture
	Strictly limited to name/contact info unless platform is licensed.

	Every listing must indicate the "last date for revision."

	Listings must be "consistent with actual status" (24h sync recommended).

	Must use the name on the license; no nicknames for the brokerage.
