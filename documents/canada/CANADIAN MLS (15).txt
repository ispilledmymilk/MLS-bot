NSREC COMPLIANCE 
1. "Coming Soon" Advertising Policy
NSREC has a formal policy for properties that are not yet on the market. If your marketplace allows users to "tease" a listing:
* The Mandatory Disclaimer: Every "Coming Soon" advertisement must include the exact phrase: "This is not an offering for sale."
* The 3-Day Cooperation Sync: Like the national CREA rule, if a property is marketed as "Coming Soon," it must be placed on the MLS® System within three business days.
* Written Authority: The marketplace must ensure the agent has a specific "Coming Soon" authorization in writing from the seller before the tag is applied.
2. Brokerage & Team Branding (The "Equal Size" Rule)
NSREC is strict about ensuring a team doesn't look like an independent brokerage.
* Size Requirements: A team name or logo cannot appear larger than the brokerage name. They must be the same size or smaller.
* Prominence: The full legal name of the brokerage must be "prominent" on every page where a listing or agent is displayed.
* Designated Agency: If your marketplace identifies agents, you must list all members of a team as the "Designated Agent" for a property, as they are legally deemed to share confidential client information.
* Reference: NSREC Designated Agency & Team FAQs
3. Sold Price Transparency (Dual Consent)
Nova Scotia has a high bar for displaying what a home actually sold for.
* The Rule: To advertise the purchase price of a property, a licensee needs written permission from both parties (the buyer and the seller).
* The VOW Solution: Similar to other provinces, the only way to show historical sold data at scale on a marketplace is through a Virtual Office Website (VOW).
   * The user must register, verify their email, and agree to a "Lawful Consumer-Broker Relationship."
   * Even with a VOW, you must display the original listing brokerage's name clearly alongside the sold data.
* Reference: NSREC Conduct & Trade Practices FAQ
4. Unlicensed Assistant Restrictions
If your marketplace has a "Chat" or "Support" feature handled by staff:
* Prohibited Activity: Unlicensed individuals cannot review, interpret, or explain listings, offers, or contracts to consumers.
* Lead Handling: They can relay factual, already-advertised listing information, but any "comparative market analysis" or advice must be directed to a licensed REALTOR®.
5. Incentives & Inducements (July 2025 Update)
As of July 1, 2025, NSREC updated the rules regarding incentives (e.g., "Buy this home and get a free iPad" or "Commission Rebates").
* Disclosure: Any incentive offered on your marketplace must include all details, terms, and conditions in writing within the advertisement.
* Broker Approval: Individual licensees can now offer their own incentives (previously it had to be brokerage-wide), but they must still have written approval from their Broker of Record.
