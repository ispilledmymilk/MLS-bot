RECO COMPLIANCE 
1. The "Identification" Rule (Bulletin 5.1)
RECO requires that any advertisement of a property clearly identifies the brokerage responsible for the listing. A "marketplace" cannot present a listing in a way that makes it look like the marketplace is the one selling it.
* Prominence: The name of the listing brokerage must be "clear and prominent." RECO often interprets this as being visible without the user having to scroll or click a "read more" button.
* Exact Legal Name: You must use the brokerage's full legal name as registered with RECO (e.g., "ABC Realty Inc., Brokerage" not just "ABC Realty").
* Team Names: If an agent/team is mentioned, the brokerage name must be more or equally prominent than the team name.
2. Consent to Advertise (Section 12.1)
You cannot simply "scrape" or display a listing because it exists on the internet.
* Written Consent: A registrant (brokerage) cannot advertise a property unless they have the written consent of the seller.
* Third-Party Platforms: When a marketplace displays a feed, the "consent" is usually baked into the IDX/DDF agreement. However, if you allow users to manually post listings, you must have a mechanism to verify they have the seller's written authority to post on a third-party site.
3. Accuracy of Status (The "Sold" Trap)
RECO is extremely strict about the "General Impression" of a listing's status.
* "Sold" vs. "Firm": You cannot advertise a property as "Sold" until the deal is firm and all conditions are waived.
* Sold Price Disclosure: Under TRESA, displaying sold prices is permitted, but RECO rules specify that you must not include anything that identifies a party (buyer/seller) or the specific contents of an agreement (like the price) unless all parties to that agreement have consented in writing.
   * Note: This is why most marketplaces hide sold prices behind a VOW login; the VOW agreement acts as the legal framework for this disclosure.
4. Prohibited Terms & False Claims
RECO monitors marketplaces for "deceptive" language that creates a false sense of scale or authority.
* "Number 1" Claims: You cannot claim to be the "#1 Marketplace" or have the "Most Listings" unless that claim is verifiable with a date-stamped source (e.g., "Based on TRREB data from Jan 2026").
* Terms of Registration: You cannot use terms like "Salesperson" or "Broker" for employees who are not actually registered with RECO.
5. The RECO Information Guide (TRESA Requirement)
As of 2024–2026, if your marketplace provides "services" to consumers (like connecting them with an agent), you have a role in ensuring the RECO Information Guide is acknowledged.
* The Rule: Agents must provide and explain the RECO Information Guide to a consumer before providing services.
* Marketplace Role: Many marketplaces now include a digital copy of the RECO Information Guide in their lead-capture forms or user dashboards to help their partner agents remain compliant.

RECO Compliance Checklist for Marketplaces
Rule
	Implementation Requirement
	Brokerage Visibility
	Is the listing brokerage name in a high-contrast, legible font?
	Registrant Names
	Are all partner agents using their "Registered Name" (no nicknames)?
	Photo Consent
	Do you have a "Terms of Use" stating that the uploader owns the rights to the photos?
	Status Accuracy
	Does your feed sync fast enough to remove "Sold" signs within 24 hours?
	Address/Price
	Are you displaying the "Sold Price" only to registered VOW users?
