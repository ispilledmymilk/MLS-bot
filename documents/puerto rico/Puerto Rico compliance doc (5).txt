5. Short-Term Rental Listing Compliance
Short-term rental (STR) listings require additional verification and disclosure steps before the platform may publish them. The platform's obligations are limited to: verifying the operator's PRTC registration, displaying the registration ID, and disclosing the 7% room tax to prospective guests.
5.1 What Qualifies as a Short-Term Rental
* Any rental of fewer than 90 consecutive days qualifies as an STR (hospedaje temporero) under Puerto Rico law.
* Long-term rentals (90+ days) are treated as standard residential rental listings — no STR-specific compliance steps apply.
* Platform obligation: collect rental duration type at listing intake. Apply STR compliance workflow only to listings offering sub-90-day rentals.

5.3 Room Occupancy Tax — 7% Disclosure
* Rate: 7% of listing price including cleaning fees. Applies to all stays of 89 nights or fewer.
* Airbnb: automatically collects and remits 7% PR Room Tax on behalf of hosts — no host action required for Airbnb bookings.
* All other OTAs and direct bookings: host is responsible for collecting from guest and remitting monthly to PRTC.
* Platform obligation: disclose the 7% room tax on all STR listings. If the platform processes direct bookings (not via Airbnb), surface the host's remittance obligation clearly in the operator dashboard.
DISCLOSURE LANGUAGE: STR listings must clearly state: 'A 7% Puerto Rico Room Occupancy Tax applies to all reservations of 89 nights or fewer. This tax is collected separately.' Platforms routing bookings through Airbnb may note that Airbnb collects this tax automatically. For all other booking methods, disclose that the host is responsible for collection and remittance.
